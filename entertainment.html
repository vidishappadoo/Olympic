Experience the Best of Paris

Paris offers a plethora of entertainment options to enrich your Olympic journey. From cultural exhibits to lively nightlife, there's something for everyone.

Eiffel Tower

A global symbol of French creativity and ingenuity, the Eiffel Tower offers:

  • Stunning views of Paris from its observation decks.
  • Iconic iron lattice tower structure recognized worldwide.
  • A romantic landmark, especially beautiful when illuminated at night.

$13 Per Person

Louvre Museum

The Louvre is not just the world's largest art museum but a historic monument in Paris. It's renowned for:

  • Home to the Mona Lisa and the Venus de Milo.
  • Spanning centuries of art, from ancient civilizations to the 19th century.
  • Architectural marvel with its iconic glass pyramid entrance.

$24 Per Person

Arc de Triomphe

Standing at the western end of the Champs-Élysées, the Arc de Triomphe is a symbol of French national pride, featuring:

  • An homage to those who fought for France.
  • Impressive sculptures and reliefs depicting French victories.
  • A panoramic terrace offering views of the Parisian skyline.

$15 Per Person

Notre Dame de Paris

An emblem of French Gothic architecture, Notre Dame stands on the Île de la Cité, featuring:

  • Stunning rose windows and Gothic sculptures.
  • The historic site of significant religious and cultural events.
  • Free entrance to appreciate the facade and public areas.

FREE

Jardin de Luxembourg

A peaceful retreat in the heart of Paris, the Luxembourg Gardens are known for:

  • Beautifully manicured lawns, fountains, and statues.
  • The Medici Fountain, a romantic spot within the gardens.
  • Spaces dedicated to children, including playgrounds and puppet shows.

FREE

Musée d'Orsay

Housed in a Beaux-Arts railway station, the Musée d'Orsay is renowned for its collection of Impressionist art, offering:

  • Masterpieces by Monet, Van Gogh, and Degas.
  • A stunning view of the Seine and across to the Louvre.
  • Unique architectural features, including the giant clock.

$18 Per Person

Palais Garnier

The opulent Palais Garnier opera house, a masterpiece of theater architecture, features:

  • Exquisite marble Grand Staircase and Chagall-painted ceiling.
  • Lavish auditorium with velvet, gold leaf, and crystal chandeliers.
  • Regularly scheduled operatic and ballet performances.

$17 Per Person

Sainte-Chapelle

A Gothic architectural gem, Sainte-Chapelle is celebrated for:

  • Its breathtaking stained glass windows that tell biblical stories.
  • The upper chapel's radiant light, creating a celestial atmosphere.
  • Its location within the medieval Palais de la Cité, the heart of Parisian power.

$15 Per Person

Musée Rodin

Dedicated to the works of Auguste Rodin, this museum offers:

  • The Thinker and The Gates of Hell among its outdoor sculptures.
  • Intimate access to Rodin's creative process in his personal studio.
  • A tranquil garden that complements the beauty of the sculptures.

$14 Per Person

Basilica of the Sacred Heart of Paris

Perched atop Montmartre, the Sacré-Cœur Basilica offers:

  • Stunning views of Paris from its highest point.
  • Iconic white domes that contribute to Paris's skyline.
  • A serene interior with one of the world's largest mosaics.

FREE

Palace of Versailles

A symbol of the absolute monarchy of the Ancien Régime, Versailles is unmatched in grandeur and scope, featuring:

  • The Hall of Mirrors, the King's Grand Apartments, and more.
  • Expansive gardens with perfectly manicured lawns, sculptures, and fountains.
  • The Trianon Palaces and the Queen's Hamlet, offering a more intimate look at royal life.

$23 Per Person

Conciergerie

Once a royal palace and prison, the Conciergerie is notable for:

  • Its role during the French Revolution as a prison for key figures, including Marie Antoinette.
  • The Hall of Soldiers, a remarkable example of Gothic architecture.
  • Exhibitions that explore its history from the Middle Ages to the present.

$18 Per Person
